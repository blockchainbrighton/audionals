Jim.BTC (Jim Crane)

Music Producer • Blockchain Pioneer • Creator of Audionals

Two Decades in Music Production

Jim Crane – known by the monikers Jim.BTC (jimdotbtc on social) and formerly by his music alias Melophonic – is a British music producer and innovator with 20+ years in the music industry. Based in Brighton, UK, he honed his craft as a producer and songwriter, contributing to diverse projects and bands. Notably, Crane was half of the Brighton-based ukulele duo Hundred Little Reasons and part of the electronic group Let The Machines Do The Work, showcasing his versatility from acoustic songwriting to dance music production.

This extensive background in music production and live performance laid the groundwork for Crane’s later innovations at the intersection of music and technology. Throughout the 2000s and 2010s, under the Melophonic alias, he built a reputation for sonic craftsmanship—experiences that would eventually inspire his foray into blockchain-based music creativity.

From Bitcoin ATM to NFT Pioneer

Crane’s curiosity and entrepreneurial spirit led him into the world of cryptocurrency early. In 2014, he made headlines by importing the UK’s first two-way Bitcoin ATM, a bold move that marked his entry into the crypto space. This pioneering encounter with Bitcoin ignited his interest in blockchain technology and its potential beyond finance.

“I was technically minded but had no idea about finance... Once I got into crypto, I started learning about code, and that learning formed into what it is now.”

By early 2021, Jim Crane co-founded This Is #1, the world’s first curated Bitcoin NFT marketplace built on the Stacks blockchain (a Bitcoin layer for smart contracts). With Crane as a driving force, This Is #1 launched a groundbreaking inaugural NFT collection—the first commercial audio-visual NFT series on Bitcoin via Stacks. This genesis collection featured one-of-one collaborations with internationally acclaimed artists: Cara Delevingne, Fatboy Slim, Orbital, and Dave Stewart (Eurythmics). The piece “Mine” by Cara Delevingne in collaboration with Chemical X became the first NFT minted and auctioned via Stacks (anchored to Bitcoin) in July 2021.

Crane and his team continued to break new ground with No.1 Smileys—a series of 1000 audio-visual NFTs created by Fatboy Slim and Chemical X for Stacks. No.1 Smileys became the first-ever music NFT collection on Stacks, each token featuring original beats and iconic smiley-themed artwork, marking the first time Fatboy Slim released music as an NFT.

Not stopping there, Jim Crane spearheaded the Hash Ones collection: the first generative art NFT collection on Stacks. 91 unique artworks were minted, each sold for just 1 STX to demonstrate the platform’s marketplace. In collaboration with Chemical X, these NFTs were procedurally generated, showcasing creative code-based art on Bitcoin.

Through This Is #1 and these early collections, Jim Crane established himself as a pioneer of Bitcoin NFTs, proving Bitcoin’s security and permanence could be harnessed for creative expression and digital collectibles.

Audionals: On-Chain Music Innovation

In 2023, Jim Crane (widely known as Jim.BTC) turned his focus fully toward Bitcoin itself—specifically the emerging Ordinals protocol, which enables content to be inscribed directly on Bitcoin. Motivated by a vision to fix the “broken” music industry model (especially around royalties and artist rights), he conceived Audionals: a suite of tools and standards for on-chain music production and distribution.

Audionals lets musicians and developers inscribe audio files and music data directly onto Bitcoin, creating Bitcoin-native digital assets. Songs, samples, and even instruments become on-chain entities, with authorship and attribution made permanently visible and immutable.

Audionals pioneered on-chain audio sample libraries with the OB1 Library, the first collection of studio-quality audio samples inscribed onto Bitcoin—each entry offering high-fidelity sound and rich metadata. Companion tools such as the Audional Sample Pad (OrdSPD) let creators play and remix these on-chain sounds live in the browser.

Building on that foundation, Jim.BTC released “TRUTH” in mid-2024—the first recursive music NFT collection on Bitcoin. The project employs recursive techniques to reference and re-use OB1’s on-chain snippets, further expanding the possibilities of blockchain-native composition.

In 2025, Jim pushed further by introducing mobile-ready, Opus-compressed audio in WebM containers—enabling album-quality, on-chain music playback at a fraction of the size and cost.

His most ambitious vision: the Bitcoin Audional Matrix (BAM)—a fully on-chain, modular digital audio workstation where every sample, synth, and plug-in is a Bitcoin-native NFT, loaded from your wallet and composed directly on the blockchain.

Jim.BTC is now fostering collaborations and new on-chain sample libraries, starting with the KP Loops project—25 exclusive drum loops by Kieron Pepper (ex-The Prodigy), inscribed on Bitcoin for the Audionals library. Each loop, with rich metadata, is fully composable in Audionals-powered apps and BAM sessions.

This community-driven approach, with optional royalties powered by benevolent marketplaces, ensures everyone—from original artist to remixer—can be recognized and fairly rewarded by tracking on-chain data across every element used, from audio samples to plug-ins, in this emerging Web3 music production ecosystem.

Legacy and Vision

From Melophonic in Brighton, to importing the first Bitcoin ATM, launching the first Stacks music NFTs, and now engineering music’s future on Bitcoin—Jim Crane’s journey is defined by cross-discipline innovation. As Jim.BTC, he bridges artistry and blockchain, achieving consistent “firsts” and shaping the foundation for a new, open creator economy.

Jim.BTC aspires to a world where artists have full autonomy over their work through decentralized tech. His tools ensure songs and sounds are “stacked” on Bitcoin and accessible to all, with collaboration and attribution baked into the platform.

As the Audionauts NFT collection and Web3 music community grow, Jim.BTC ensures music on the blockchain becomes a revolution—not just an experiment—in how art is created, owned, and experienced.
