TB-303 ACID SYNTH

Sync Setup Guide

Use a Web MIDI capable browser such as Chrome or Edge. When using Ableton Live or another DAW, route its MIDI clock to a virtual MIDI port that the browser can read.

  1. Enable a virtual MIDI output (macOS: turn on an IAC Driver bus in Audio MIDI Setup. Windows: create a loopMIDI or similar virtual port).
  2. Open your DAW preferences and enable Sync (and optionally Start/Stop) on that MIDI output.
  3. Back in the browser, click MIDI Clock in this app and grant access to the virtual port. The status light will confirm the connection.
  4. Press play or start the clock in your DAW. The browser will follow the incoming tempo once the first bar of MIDI clock arrives.
  • Keep the DAW transport running to maintain lock; stopping the DAW pauses tempo updates.
  • If nothing shows up, re-open this panel and double-check the virtual MIDI port is enabled and selected.
