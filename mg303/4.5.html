üîó Why Not Ableton Link?

You may notice that this synth does not support Ableton Link. That‚Äôs because Link requires direct access to your local network clock, which browsers intentionally restrict for security reasons. While native desktop apps can freely use Link to share tempo across devices on Wi-Fi or LAN, browsers run inside a sandbox and cannot access the same networking layer in real-time.

Instead, browser-based instruments rely on Web MIDI + virtual MIDI drivers to receive timing information. This allows synchronization, but it‚Äôs limited to MIDI Clock rather than Link‚Äôs peer-to-peer protocol.

In short: Link won‚Äôt work in a browser‚Äîuse MIDI Clock for reliable sync.

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting

No MIDI devices found:

  • Make sure your virtual MIDI driver is installed and running
  • Refresh the browser page after setting up the virtual MIDI port
  • Check that your browser has MIDI permissions

Connected but not syncing:

  • Make sure your DAW is playing (not just armed)
  • Verify MIDI Clock output is enabled in your DAW's MIDI settings
  • Check that "Sync" is enabled, not just "Track" or "Remote"

Browser compatibility:

  • Chrome, Edge, and Opera support Web MIDI
  • Firefox and Safari require additional setup or don't support Web MIDI
  • Use Chrome-based browsers for best results
