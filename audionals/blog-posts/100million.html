How We Got to 100 M

The 100 millionth Ordinal inscription landed in block 846 912 on 15 June 2025 according to multiple Dune dashboards[1][2]. That comes just 867 days after Casey Rodarmor released the first version of ord in January 2023[10].

The Evolving Media Mix

Ordinal MIME‑type data show a steady shift from text and static images toward video, 3‑D and, most notably, audio. The current breakdown is:

  • Text / JSON — ≈46 % (BRC‑20 & token metadata)
  • Still images — ≈29 % (PNG, JPEG, AVIF)
  • Video — ≈11 % (WebM, MP4)
  • Interactive — ≈6 % (HTML, JavaScript apps)
  • 3‑D & Metaverse — ≈5 % (GLB, BRC‑420 bundles)[3]
  • Audio — ≈3 % but fastest‑growing segment[3][5]

Standards such as BRC‑69 recursively compress large files by more than 90 %, while BRC‑420 adds royalty & licensing fields—both crucial for heavy media like music and video[4][9].

Block‑Space Economics

With fees back under 2 sat/vB in June 2025, a full 4 MB album now costs <$150 to inscribe—down from >$1 000 during the 2024 Runes frenzy[3][8].

Meanwhile miner revenue from inscriptions has fallen below 1 % of total rewards, despite Bitcoin’s hash‑rate reaching new highs[8]. Low fees are a boon for creators but renew the debate over long‑term security budgets.

Developer Checklist

  • Upgrade to ord 0.23.2 for JPEG‑XL and improved Runes handling[8].
  • Index with Hiro’s Ordhook for re‑org resilience.
  • Use BRC‑69 for anything over 100 KB; fall back to plain recursion for bit‑perfect audio.
  • Add BRC‑420 metadata if you need royalty splits.

FAQ

How big can an audio file be?
Technically up to ~4 MB in a single inscription, but recursion lets you chain unlimited chunks.
Do I need a special wallet?
Xverse, Leather, OKX and UniSat all support audio playback as of Q2 2025.
What about royalties?
BRC‑420 supports on‑chain royalty splits; marketplaces like Magic Eden read this field automatically.
