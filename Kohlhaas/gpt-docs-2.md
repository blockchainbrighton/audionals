# <span style="color:#6A5ACD;">1. Project Overview</span>

### <span style="color:#4682B4;">Purpose & Objectives</span>

The Brighton Festival is planning a large-scale production in the Corn Exchange requiring **very wide theatrical gauze (scrim)**. Since commercial scrims such as *GobelinTulle* or *Cyclo 270* reach widths only around **10â€“12 m**, wider surfaces must be built by seaming multiple panels.

To determine the correct material, the team needs **hundreds of metres of cloth for in-situ testing**. To avoid unnecessary purchasing, the project aims to **borrow or acquire retired/off-cut gauze** from other organisations, test densities and weave types, and purchase new stock only once the design is confirmed.

---

### <span style="color:#4682B4;">Sustainability Ambitions</span>

Sustainability is a core project value. Following guidance from the **Theatre Green Book**, which encourages reuse and material exchange, the project will:

* Source **decommissioned scrim** rather than buying new
* Divert material from landfill
* Reduce carbon emissions
* Build relationships with reuse hubs and suppliers

A **circular end-of-life plan** ensures all gauze â€” borrowed or purchased â€” remains out of landfill (see *Onward Chain Plan* below).

---

### <span style="color:#4682B4;">Outreach Strategy Summary</span>

**1. Engage reuse networks**
Set-Exchange, Re-Set Scenery, Scenery Salvage, Resource Hub and others can supply ex-rental or decommissioned scrim.

**2. Leverage community forums**
Production Managers Forum, Set-Exchange message boards and Facebook groups.

**3. Partner with suppliers**
ShowTex, J&C Joel, Flints, Stage Electrics â€” with interest in off-cuts and ex-rental stock. ShowTex lines (GobelinTulle, Cyclo 270) provide technical benchmarks.
