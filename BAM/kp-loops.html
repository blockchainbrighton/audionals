About Kieron Pepper

Kieron Pepper is a multi-instrumentalist and former live drummer for electronic music pioneers The Prodigy (1997–2007), renowned for his powerful, high-energy performances. A current member of the legendary BBC Radiophonic Workshop—most recently collaborating with Spitfire Audio—Kieron is also the bass player for Britpop icons Sleeper, co-writer and co-producer with Wargasm, and Director of Artist Development at WaterBear College of Music.

Now an accomplished producer and dedicated educator, Kieron brings decades of rhythmic innovation to the Bitcoin Audional Matrix (BAM) with this exclusive set of 25 brand-new, electrifying drum loops designed for the future of on-chain music creation.

Shape the Collection: Your Bids Decide!

Get ready for an unprecedented fusion of music creation and community curation! Instead of releasing a fixed set, YOU get to decide the final sound of the Kieron Pepper Drum Loop Library.

For each of the 25 foundational drum patterns, Kieron has produced several distinct variations. You can explore these variants above. Now, here's where it gets exciting:

  • Bid on Your Favorites: Users will place bids on the specific variant they want to see included in the final, on-chain collection for each of the 25 loop slots.
  • Highest Bid Wins: For each loop number (1 through 25), the variant receiving the single highest bid will be declared the winner.
  • Immortalized On-Chain: Only these 25 winning variants – chosen by the community's collective bids – will be inscribed as Bitcoin Ordinals using the Audionals protocol. They become the definitive Kieron Pepper x BAM collection, forever etched onto the blockchain.

This is your chance to actively participate in making history. Listen closely, choose wisely, and place your bids to help curate this unique, immutable library of Kieron Pepper's signature drumming!

Immutable & On-Chain

The 25 winning loop variants packaged as JSON-based Audionals assets, inscribed on satoshis via Ordinals protocol for verifiable ownership and on-chain playback.
